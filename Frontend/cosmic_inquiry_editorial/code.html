The Spooky Action

Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance." Quantum entanglement posits that two particles, once interacting, remain intimately connected regardless of the distance separating them. A change in the state of one instantaneously affects the other, defying the classical limits of light speed and spatial separation.

Yet, within ancient mystical traditions, this concept is not novel. The idea that "all is one"—that separation is merely an illusion of perception—forms the core of contemplative spirituality from Sufi mysticism to Vedantic philosophy.

The Inner Observer

If observation collapses the wave function, bringing definite state out of probability, what is the nature of the observer? The 'hard problem of consciousness' interfaces directly with quantum measurement.

We propose a framework where consciousness is not an emergent property of matter, but a fundamental field—akin to gravity or electromagnetism—that interacts via entangled nodes within biological neural networks.
